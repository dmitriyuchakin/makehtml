Headline: Walking the walk: American’s Safety Operations Partners

Description: Meet two Safety Operations Partners (SOPs): Sarah Kassebaum and Chaz Adams.

Topic: Safety

During National Safety Month, we’re highlighting American’s Safety Operations Partners (SOPs), a unique function of the Safety Team with a “boots on the ground” approach.

The newly-created SOP structure embeds members of the Safety team in the operation. They’re focused on collaborating with frontline team members on safety best practices, identifying risks, conducting investigations, offering solutions to challenges in the field and building and reinforcing a safety-focused workplace. There are 22 SOPs spread across the U.S., supporting every hub, several Tech Ops locations and international operations.

Today, we introduce you to two SOPs: Sarah Kassebaum, Tech Ops – Tulsa, and Chaz Adams, LAX.

SUBHED: Meet Sarah Kassebaum, SOP at Tech Ops – Tulsa

Sarah Kassebaum started with American as a 19-year-old who took a night job cleaning Tech Ops – Tulsa so she could go to school during the day. Almost 25 years later, Sarah is the SOP and a fixture of the safety culture at the 330-acre base, the largest commercial aviation maintenance base in the world.

PULL QUOTE: “I wasn’t looking for a career in safety — it kind of found me — and I am so thankful it did,” Sarah said.

To help her colleagues see safety through a different lens, Sarah takes everyday examples of observations at the base and shares them regularly at leadership and townhall meetings to start honest conversations about safety. The examples could be as simple as a water leak in the middle of a walkway — a potential slip hazard — or as impactful as not wearing proper personal protective equipment, which could lead to a life-altering injury.

“When I share these pictures, it helps connect the dots for people about why safety needs to be at the forefront of everything we do, every single day,” she said. “It’s not about punishment — it’s about learning what we as a team, both leaders and floor members, can do better.”

But she doesn’t just talk the talk. Sarah also takes regular walks through the base with the base’s leadership to identify any areas of opportunity that could make the workplace safer and be a visible, reliable presence.

“Actions often change attitudes,” she said. “And showing our team that we are out there in the operation, identifying concerns, and helping fix them, no matter how big or small, adds to overall safety awareness and willingness to change.”

That’s no small feat, considering there are more than 5,000 team members working at the base.

“At the end of the day, the whole reason I do this job and care the way I do is because I really want everyone who works here to be able to live the life they want to live with their friends and family outside of the job.”

SUBHED: Meet Chaz Adams, SOP at LAX

If you ask Chaz Adams about his career in safety, he’ll tell you that it’s been a team effort from day one.

“I attribute my eye for safety to being a middle child — learning from my older and younger siblings,” he said with a laugh.

Second to his family, Chaz says it was the encouragement of several leaders that led him to pursue a career in safety. In 2014, while working for another airline at their Hub Control Center, Chaz would identify risks he saw on camera and bring his concerns to his manager. That same manager gave Chaz a leadership opportunity and made him a safety chair, helping to further amplify his passion for safety.

“Going from being a bug in his ear to being given that opportunity to be a part of a bigger outcome — that was really awesome,” Chaz said.

In 2021, Chaz started his career at American as a customer service manager (CSM) and credits his then-leader, Faith Ikeda, with getting him involved in safety at American.

“She saw in me a passion for safety,” Chaz said. “She quickly gave me opportunities to get more exposure through training, TDYs (temporary duty), and collaborating with other teams to develop safety materials for airports.”

Today, Chaz is a Safety Operations Partner (SOP) at LAX, a role he’s had for just six months but one in which he feels right at home.

PULL QUOTE: “We always have something we can learn and share, and it feels like this [being an SOP] is where I belong.”

“At its foundation, [being an SOP is] all about establishing relationships,” Chaz said. “Then once those relationships are in place, we really have the opportunity to influence outcomes and build safety culture. That’s where the action is.”

His advice for team members who are interested in learning more about safety?

“Attend a safety meeting. Reach out and connect with your SOP to go for a walk or grab a cup of coffee. Even if you don’t know where to start, we’re here to help.”
